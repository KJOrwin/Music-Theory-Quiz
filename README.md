# Music Theory Quiz
As a part of the Computer Science module (COMP1004) at my university, we are required to make a Single Page Application (SPA). For this course I have decided to make a music theory quiz and teaching resource so that people can learn basic music notation and sheet music for the piano.

This project tests your ability to read sheet music and your note identification skills by giving you a bar of four notes at a time and you are tasked with identifying them by inputting them using your keyboard:

|Note|low C|C#|D|Eb|E|F|F#|G|Ab|A|Bb|B|high C|
|-|-|-|-|-|-|-|-|-|-|-|-|-|-|
|Key|s|e|d|r|f|g|y|h|u|j|i|k|l|

Sight reading exercises provide four notes on a stave and expect you to repeat them back while ear training exercises will give you the audio of four notes and expect you to repeat them back.

If the exercises are too easy or too dificult then you can use the two togglable options to either enable or disable accidentals (black notes) from appearing in questions or make mistakes immediately fail the question or be counted but allow you to continue.

Any questions that you find particularly difficult can be appended to a file which can be downloaded to your computer. These files can contain multiple questions and after importing it back into the program, it will repeat the questions in a quiz style format and will show you how well you completed the questions by giving you a score at the end.

Hope you enjoy learning music with my project and happy practicing!
